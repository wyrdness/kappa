# Sources & References

## Academic & Scholarly Works

### Yokai Studies
- **Foster, M.D.** (2009). *Pandemonium and Parade: Japanese Monsters and the Culture of Yokai*. University of California Press. ISBN: 978-0520253629
  - Comprehensive academic study of yokai in Japanese culture
  - Essential for understanding kappa in cultural context

- **Foster, M.D.** (2015). *The Book of Yokai: Mysterious Creatures of Japanese Folklore*. University of California Press. ISBN: 978-0520271029
  - Modern yokai encyclopedia
  - Detailed kappa entry

- **Reider, N.T.** (2010). *Japanese Demon Lore: Oni from Ancient Times to the Present*. Utah State University Press. ISBN: 978-0874217891
  - Academic analysis of Japanese demons and yokai
  - Cultural and historical context

### Japanese Folklore Collections
- **Komatsu, K.** (Ed.) (2017). *An Introduction to Yōkai Culture: Monsters, Ghosts, and Outsiders in Japanese History*. Japan Publishing Industry Foundation for Culture.
  - Academic introduction to yokai culture

- **Mizuki, S.** (Multiple works). Various yokai encyclopedias and manga
  - Famous yokai researcher and manga artist
  - Definitive visual representations

## Classic Japanese Sources

### Edo Period Texts
- *Gazu Hyakki Yagyō* (画図百鬼夜行) — "The Illustrated Night Parade of a Hundred Demons" by Toriyama Sekien
  - Classic yokai illustration collection
  - Kappa depictions

- *Wakan Sansai Zue* (和漢三才図会) — Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia (1712)
  - Early encyclopedic reference to kappa

### Folk Tale Collections
- Various regional *mukashibanashi* (old tales) collections
- Local folklore documenting regional kappa variations
